A selection of research projects from my graduate and internship work, spanning
solid-state batteries, machine-learning interatomic potentials, and organic
electronics.

Solid electrolyte interphase for anode-free batteries
======
*Henkelman Group, University of Texas at Austin · 2022 – Present*

Investigated the role of a lithiophilic interphase layer (Li₂Te) in lithium
deposition on a copper current collector for stable anode-free all-solid-state
lithium batteries. Used DFT to assess the thermodynamic stability of continuous
Li electrodeposition on passivated surfaces through binding-energy comparison.

Halide-based electrolytes for all-solid-state batteries
======
*Henkelman Group, University of Texas at Austin · 2022 – Present*

Studied entropic effects on ionic transport and screened new cathode materials
using quantum-mechanical and accelerated-dynamics methods (adaptive kinetic
Monte Carlo and machine-learning molecular dynamics). Quantified anharmonic
effects — thermal expansion and internal-energy changes in defect migration —
and resolved sub-lattice ordering effects on diffusion in superionic conductors.

Phase evolution in boron nitride from large-scale ML molecular dynamics
======
*Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory · Jun – Dec 2024*

Developed and validated a MACE machine-learning potential for boron-nitride
polymorphs, then applied machine-learning molecular dynamics, enhanced sampling,
and solid-state NEB to quantify energy barriers and interfacial evolution
pathways between distinct BN phases — advancing the understanding of BN
polymorphism and phase stability.

Organic-ligand effects on nanoparticles for PEMFCs
======
*Korea Institute of Science and Technology · Jun – Dec 2020*

Used DFT to study non-stoichiometric CeOₓ nanoparticles as radical scavengers
for long-term-stable polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cells, evaluating the
binding energetics of radical species (•OH and •OOH) and the electronic origin
of enhanced radical-scavenging activity via density-of-states analysis.

Molecular library of OLED host materials
======
*Max Planck Institute for Polymer Research · Jul – Oct 2019*

Performed quantum-mechanical and molecular-dynamics simulations to compute the
electronic properties of organic molecules and analyze structure–property
relationships, identifying critical parameters for high conductivity and
contributing to a public molecular database for OLED host materials.

Modeling charge transport in organic hosts for OLEDs
======
*Seoul National University · Jan 2018 – Jan 2020*

Combined DFT, molecular dynamics with a polarizable force field, and kinetic
Monte Carlo to extract charge-carrier mobility in organic host materials and
identify the structural origins of mobility differences across molecular
substituents.
