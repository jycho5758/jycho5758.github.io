I apply accelerated atomistic simulation to the materials and interfaces at the
heart of next-generation solid-state batteries, connecting atomic-scale
mechanisms to macroscopic performance.

Solid-state electrolytes & structure–property relationships
======
Resolving ion-diffusion mechanisms in halide-type Li⁺/Na⁺ conductors — including
the superionic transition, anharmonic effects, and sub-lattice ordering — to
derive design rules for highly conductive solid-state electrolytes.

Phase transformation of battery materials
======
Uncovering the atomistic mechanisms and kinetic pathways of phase
transformations — for example, phase evolution in boron nitride from large-scale
machine-learning molecular dynamics — that determine material stability and
processing.

Anode-free battery systems
======
Studying lithiophilic interphase layers (e.g. Li₂Te) and metal wetting on copper
current collectors to enable stable, high-energy-density anode-free
all-solid-state lithium batteries.
