I develop and apply methods that extend atomistic simulation to the length and
time scales where the interesting physics of ion transport and phase
transformation actually happens — well beyond the reach of conventional
molecular dynamics.

Machine-learning interatomic potentials
======
Building and validating **MACE** machine-learning potentials that reproduce
near-DFT accuracy at a fraction of the cost, enabling large-scale, long-time
simulations of both ordered and disordered materials.

Long-timescale dynamics
======
Using **adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo (AKMC)** and **parallel replica dynamics**
to capture rare-event diffusion and reach the timescales relevant to real
battery operation.

Transition-state search
======
Applying the **Dimer method**, **nudged elastic band (NEB)**, and **solid-state
NEB** to locate saddle points and map the energy barriers and pathways that
govern kinetics.

Enhanced sampling
======
Employing **metadynamics** and **umbrella sampling** to reconstruct free-energy
landscapes of phase transformations and activated processes.
