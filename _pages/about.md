I am a Ph.D. candidate in Chemistry at the [University of Texas at Austin](https://www.utexas.edu/), working with [Prof. Graeme Henkelman](https://theory.cm.utexas.edu/henkelman/). My research uses computational chemistry to understand the **thermodynamic and kinetic properties of materials for energy storage**, with a focus on solid-state battery materials.

I develop and apply **accelerated atomistic simulation methods** — machine-learning interatomic potentials, adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo, and enhanced sampling — to study ion diffusion and phase transformation mechanisms beyond the reach of conventional molecular dynamics. I expect to graduate in Fall 2026.

Research interests
======
- **Accelerated dynamics & sampling methods** — machine-learning interatomic potentials (MACE), long-timescale dynamics (adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo, parallel replica dynamics), and transition-state search (Dimer, NEB, solid-state NEB).
- **Materials & systems for energy storage** — design rules for solid-state electrolytes and structure–property relationships, phase transformation of battery materials, and anode-free battery systems.

I have carried out research at the [Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory](https://www.llnl.gov/), the [Korea Institute of Science and Technology](https://www.kist.re.kr/), and the [Max Planck Institute for Polymer Research](https://www.mpip-mainz.mpg.de/), and I collaborate actively with experimental and computational groups in the US, UK, and Germany.
