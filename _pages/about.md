    <div style="line-height:1.7; font-size:1.08em; color:#333;">
      <p style="margin-bottom:22px;">
        I am a Ph.D. candidate in Chemistry at the <a href="https://www.utexas.edu/">University of Texas at Austin</a>,
        working with <a href="https://theory.cm.utexas.edu/henkelman/"><strong>Prof. Graeme Henkelman</strong></a>.
        My research uses computational chemistry to understand the thermodynamic and kinetic properties of materials
        for energy storage, with a focus on solid-state battery materials.
      </p>
      <p style="margin-bottom:22px;">
        I develop and apply accelerated atomistic simulation methods — machine-learning interatomic potentials,
        adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo, and enhanced sampling — to study ion diffusion and phase transformation
        mechanisms beyond the reach of conventional molecular dynamics. I expect to graduate in Fall 2026.
      </p>

      <h2 style="font-size:1.7em; color:#1a1a1a; margin-top:40px; margin-bottom:10px; font-weight:400;">research interests</h2>
      <ul style="list-style:none; padding:0; color:#666; font-size:1.02em; line-height:1.8;">
        <li>• <strong>Accelerated dynamics &amp; sampling:</strong> machine-learning interatomic potentials (MACE),
          adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo, enhanced sampling, transition-state search.</li>
        <li>• <strong>Energy-storage materials:</strong> solid-state electrolytes and structure–property
          relationships, phase transformation, anode-free batteries.</li>
      </ul>

Experience
======
* **Graduate Research Assistant**, Henkelman Group, University of Texas at Austin (Aug 2022 – Present)
  * Solid electrolyte interphase design for anode-free all-solid-state lithium batteries (DFT).
  * Diffusion and entropic effects in halide solid-state electrolytes (adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo, machine-learning molecular dynamics).

* **Computational Chemistry & Materials Science Graduate Intern**, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (Jun – Dec 2024)
  * Developed and validated a MACE machine-learning potential for boron-nitride polymorphs; quantified phase-transformation pathways via enhanced sampling and solid-state NEB.

* **Full-Time Research Intern**, Korea Institute of Science and Technology (Jun – Dec 2020)
  * DFT study of organic-ligand effects on CeOₓ nanoparticles as radical scavengers for PEMFCs.

* **Visiting Research Student**, Max Planck Institute for Polymer Research (Jul – Oct 2019)
  * Quantum-mechanical and molecular-dynamics modeling of OLED host materials for a public molecular database.

* **Graduate Research Assistant**, Seoul National University (Jan 2018 – Jan 2020)
  * Charge-transport modeling in organic hosts using DFT, polarizable-force-field MD, and kinetic Monte Carlo.
