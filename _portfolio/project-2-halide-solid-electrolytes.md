## Motivation
Halide-based solid electrolytes are promising superionic conductors, but their
diffusion mechanisms unfold over timescales far beyond conventional molecular
dynamics. **What governs fast ion transport — and can we design for it?**

## Approach
I combine quantum-mechanical calculations with accelerated-dynamics methods —
**adaptive kinetic Monte Carlo (AKMC)** and **machine-learning molecular
dynamics** (MACE potentials) — to:

- quantify anharmonic effects (thermal expansion and internal-energy changes in
  defect migration),
- resolve sub-lattice ordering effects on diffusion, and
- study the superionic transition in halide-type Li⁺ and Na⁺ conductors, with the
  goal of tuning the critical temperature toward practical operating conditions.

I am also revisiting the origin of the **Meyer–Neldel rule** to derive design
principles for highly conductive solid-state ion conductors.
