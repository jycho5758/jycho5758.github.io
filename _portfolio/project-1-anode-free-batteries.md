## Motivation
Anode-free batteries maximize energy density by plating lithium directly onto a
bare copper current collector, but uneven, unstable deposition limits their
cycle life. This project asks a mechanistic question: **how does the chemistry of
the interphase layer control where and how lithium deposits?**

## Approach
Using density functional theory (DFT), I evaluated the thermodynamic stability of
continuous Li electrodeposition on passivated copper surfaces through
binding-energy comparisons, and studied how coating layers — such as Li₂Te and
bilayer Mg/W — tune the lithiophilicity of the current collector.

## Outcomes
This work, in collaboration with **Prof. David Mitlin's group**, contributed to
two publications in *Advanced Materials* / *Advanced Energy Materials* on stable
anode-free all-solid-state lithium batteries.
